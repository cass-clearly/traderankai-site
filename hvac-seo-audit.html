Google Business Profile
GBP · 1 of 5
Is your Google Business Profile fully filled out? (description, categories, hours, phone, website, service area)
Why it matters
Google uses your GBP data to decide if your business is relevant for local searches. An incomplete profile signals low credibility — Google may not show you at all. Filling out every field takes 30 minutes and has a direct, documented impact on local pack rankings.
GBP · 2 of 5
Do you have at least 20 reviews — and are you actively getting new ones?
Why it matters
Reviews are one of Google's top 3 local ranking factors. Quantity matters, but so does recency — a business with 50 reviews from 2021 looks stagnant. Aim for a steady drip of new reviews every month. One easy tactic: text customers a Google review link right after a job.
GBP · 3 of 5
Have you posted on GBP in the last 7 days?
Why it matters
GBP posts keep your profile active and signal to Google that the business is operating. Posts expire after 7 days, which means competitors who post weekly are consistently fresher than you. It takes 5 minutes to post a job photo, a seasonal tip, or a special offer.
GBP · 4 of 5
Do your GBP photos show actual work — not stock photos?
Why it matters
Real photos of your trucks, team, and completed jobs build trust and convert searchers into callers. Profiles with 100+ photos get significantly more direction requests. Stock photos signal that you have no real presence. Even a dozen phone snapshots of finished jobs is miles ahead of nothing.
GBP · 5 of 5
Is your NAP (name, address, phone) identical across Google, Yelp, and your website?
Why it matters
NAP consistency is a local SEO signal. If Google sees "Joe's HVAC LLC" on your site, "Joe's HVAC" on Yelp, and "Joe's Heating & Cooling" on Angi — that confusion undermines trust in your listing. Even minor differences (Suite vs Ste, spelled-out vs abbreviation) matter. Check all three and make them exactly match.
🌐
Your Website
Website · 1 of 5
Does your homepage title tag include your city and primary service?
Why it matters
The title tag is the single most weighted on-page SEO signal. If yours says "Home | Welcome to Our Site" instead of "AC Repair & HVAC Service | Denver, CO | CoolBreeze Heating," you're invisible in organic search. Check it by right-clicking your homepage → View Page Source → search for <title>.
Website · 2 of 5
Do you have a dedicated page for each major service?
Why it matters
Google can't rank a single page for "AC repair," "furnace install," "water heater replacement," and "drain cleaning" all at once. Each service needs its own URL, its own title tag, and its own content. One-page sites or "Services" pages that list everything are SEO dead ends for competitive terms.
Website · 3 of 5
Does your site load in under 3 seconds on mobile?
Why it matters
Google uses mobile-first indexing — your mobile speed score directly affects rankings. More importantly, 60% of local service searches happen on phones. A slow site loses the lead before the page even loads. Check for free at pagespeed.web.dev — look for a score of 70+ on mobile.
Website · 4 of 5
Is your phone number clickable on mobile?
Why it matters
Local customers want to call, not fill out a form. If your phone number is plain text instead of a tap-to-call link (<a href="tel:...">), you're losing calls on every mobile visit. Test it right now on your phone. This takes a developer 2 minutes to fix.
Website · 5 of 5
Does your website have any blog posts or articles?
Why it matters
Blog content is how you capture the hundreds of questions homeowners type before they even know they need a technician: "why is my AC blowing warm air," "how long do water heaters last," "signs of a gas leak." Each article is a new page Google can rank. Even 4–5 good articles per year compounds over time.
🔍
Content & Keywords
Keywords · 1 of 3
Does any page on your site mention "[your city] + your service" in the body text?
Why it matters
Google matches the words on your pages to what people type. If nobody on your site has ever written "furnace repair in Colorado Springs," Google has no signal to rank you for it. Your city name + service keywords need to appear naturally in your page copy, headings, and meta descriptions — not stuffed, just present.
Keywords · 2 of 3
Do you rank on the first page for "[your main service] + [your city]"?
Why it matters
This is the bottom-line check. Open an incognito window (or use a VPN to your city) and search "[your service] [your city]." If you're not on page 1 — in the map pack or organic results — you essentially don't exist for that query. Check how many competitors are above you and which of these checklist items they have that you don't.
Keywords · 3 of 3
Have you ever targeted "near me" search terms?
Why it matters
"Plumber near me" and "HVAC repair near me" are some of the highest-intent searches in local services — and they're exploding. Google resolves "near me" to your actual location, so you can't just stuff it everywhere. But having a page that says "looking for a trusted plumber near you in [City]?" helps signal relevance. Your GBP also heavily influences these results.
🏆
Authority & Trust
Authority · 1 of 2
Does your website have any backlinks from local sources?
Why it matters
Links from other websites to yours are votes of authority. Local links — local newspaper, chamber of commerce, neighborhood blog, local charity you sponsor — carry extra weight for local SEO. Check what you have for free at ahrefs.com/free-backlink-checker. Even 5–10 good local links can separate you from competitors.
Authority · 2 of 2
Is your site listed in major directories? (Yelp, BBB, Angi, Thumbtack)
Why it matters
Major directories (Yelp, BBB, Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor) do two things: they send direct leads, and they pass "citation authority" to your business — reinforcing your NAP data for Google. They also rank well themselves, meaning your listing might appear on page 1 even if your site doesn't yet. Free to create; zero reason not to be in all of them.
